Adaptive Reuse

Honoring the narrative of historic structures by reimagining their utility for the modern era—transforming legacy assets into vibrant centers of civic life.

Fort Sullivan

Known as the "Mugdock beach castle," this unique property at the edge of Fort Moultrie National Historic Park breathes new life into a c. 1891 chapel. By integrating the historic stone structure with the modern Mugdock Keep addition, the project serves as a comfortable bastion of hospitality and a testament to the enduring beauty of coastal vernacular design.

St. James

The restoration of the historic c. 1858 St. James Methodist Church represents a milestone in downtown Charleston’s adaptive landscape. This ambitious project preserves a landmark structure while introducing an elegant event venue, neighborhood café, and five luxury residential rentals—fostering a new destination where history meets community gathering.

414 Whilden

A masterclass in small-scale civic revitalization. This unassuming commercial building within the Earl’s Court neighborhood was meticulously renovated to house two vibrant artist galleries. The conversion demonstrates how strategic reuse can anchor a neighborhood’s cultural identity, proving that even the most modest structures can be elevated into essential civic assets.
